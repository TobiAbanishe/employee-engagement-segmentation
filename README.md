# Project 6: Employee Engagement Segmentation Dashboard

## Overview

This project segments 900 employees into Gallup's three engagement tiers, identifies the structural drivers of disengagement, surfaces flight risk profiles, and quantifies the business cost of low engagement. The analysis follows the methodology established in Gallup's Q12 framework and their State of the Global Workplace report, applying it to a synthetic dataset calibrated to published global benchmarks.

The project demonstrates applied People Analytics skills across survey data analysis, segmentation, manager-level reporting, and business impact quantification — making it directly relevant to People Analytics, HRIS, and HR Analyst roles.

---

## Research Foundation

### Gallup Q12 Employee Engagement Survey
The Q12 is a 12-item survey developed by Gallup through decades of research across 2.7 million employees in 96 countries. Each item is rated on a 1 to 5 scale (1 = strongly disagree, 5 = strongly agree). The items are grouped into four psychological needs: basic needs (Q01, Q02), individual needs (Q03, Q04, Q05, Q06), teamwork needs (Q07, Q08, Q09, Q10), and growth needs (Q11, Q12). Items Q04, Q05, Q06, Q07, and Q11 are most sensitive to manager behaviour.

### Gallup State of the Global Workplace Report (2023)
Key findings that shape this project:

Globally, only 23% of employees are engaged, 59% are not engaged (quietly quitting), and 18% are actively disengaged. The cost of global disengagement totals $8.8 trillion, or approximately 9% of global GDP. Manager quality accounts for approximately 70% of the variance in team engagement scores. Engaged employees show 81% lower absenteeism and 23% higher profitability than their actively disengaged counterparts.

### Engagement Mechanics

The data generator encodes two structural patterns consistent with Gallup research:

First, a manager pool of 60 managers is created, each with a quality score drawn from a beta distribution. Manager quality is passed to each employee's Q12 generation, with manager-heavy items (Q04, Q05, Q06, Q07, Q11) weighted at 70% manager influence and environment items at 40%. This directly replicates Gallup's finding that manager quality explains 70% of team engagement variance.

Second, a tenure effect is applied: new hires (under 6 months) receive a positive honeymoon adjustment of +0.30 on their base score, employees at 6 to 24 months receive a slight positive adjustment as reality sets in, and employees over 10 years follow a bimodal pattern where 55% receive a negative adjustment and 45% receive a positive one, reflecting Gallup's long-tenure bifurcation finding.

## How to Run

### Generate the dataset

```bash
pip install pandas numpy openpyxl faker
python engagement_generator.py
```

This creates `engagement_data.csv` and `engagement_data.xlsx` in the same directory.

### Run the notebook

```bash
pip install jupyter matplotlib seaborn scipy
jupyter notebook engagement_notebook.ipynb
```

### Run the SQL queries

Load `engagement_data.csv` into a PostgreSQL database as the `engagement_data` table, then execute the queries in `engagement_analysis.sql`. All queries are compatible with PostgreSQL 13 and above.

### Connect to Power BI

1. Open Power BI Desktop and import `engagement_data.xlsx` or `engagement_data.csv`.
2. Open the Modeling tab and create a New Measure.
3. Paste each measure from `engagement_dax_measures.txt` into the formula bar.

## Power BI Dashboard Pages

### Page 1: Executive Summary
KPI cards for overall engaged %, not engaged %, actively disengaged %, flight risk count, and average absenteeism days. A donut chart shows segment mix. A call-out compares the engaged % to the Gallup 2023 global benchmark of 23%.

### Page 2: Department Segmentation
Horizontal bar chart ranking departments by average Q12 score, coloured by engagement band. Stacked bar showing segment mix for each department. Slicer for department and job level.

### Page 3: Q12 Item Analysis
Heatmap of all 12 Q12 item averages by department. A separate bar chart ranks all 12 items from weakest to strongest across the full organisation. Manager-heavy items are labelled. Used to identify targeted intervention themes.

### Page 4: Manager and Flight Risk Analysis
Scatter plot of manager quality score vs. team average Q12 score with regression line. Table ranking managers by team engagement score and flight risk count. Drill-through to individual flight risk roster.

### Page 5: Business Impact
Side-by-side bar charts showing absenteeism, performance rating, and intent to stay by engagement segment. A call-out card shows the absenteeism reduction potential if disengaged employees moved to the engaged tier. eNPS breakdown by Promoter, Passive, and Detractor.

## Key Findings (from Notebook)

The dataset produces an engagement distribution approximating Gallup's global benchmark: approximately 23% Engaged, 59% Not Engaged, and 18% Actively Disengaged.

The manager quality correlation with team average Q12 score produces an R-squared of approximately 0.45 to 0.55, directionally consistent with Gallup's 70% manager variance finding. The dataset is intentionally moderate rather than exact because real-world data never cleanly isolates a single variable.

The absenteeism analysis shows an approximately 75 to 80% reduction from the actively disengaged average to the engaged average, consistent with Gallup's published 81% benchmark.

Customer Support and Operations consistently rank as the lowest-engagement departments in the generated data, reflecting Gallup research showing frontline and service roles face the greatest engagement challenges. Q04 (Recognition) and Q10 (Best friend at work) consistently score lowest across the organisation.
